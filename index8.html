Curatorial Philosophy

Noire is not a gallery of objects, but of positions. Each work exists in dialogue with darkness — not as absence, but as presence restrained.

Darkness, within Noire, is not a void. It is a deliberate reduction — a refusal of excess. In removing noise, form becomes unavoidable. Meaning becomes slower, heavier, and more intimate.

Selected Works

To look at art is not to consume it. It is to stand still long enough for it to look back.
